Governing Body

Executive, Administrative & Decision-Making Authority

The Governing Body shall be the principal executive authority of the Trust and shall be responsible for day-to-day administration, expeditious decision-making, and implementation of the Trust's core benevolent functions.

1 General Powers and Administration

  • ✓To administer and manage the affairs of the Trust in accordance with the Trust Deed.
  • ✓To implement the objects of the Trust efficiently and compassionately.
  • ✓To operate bank accounts and manage funds as authorized under the Trust Deed.

2 Committees

  • ✓To constitute, reconstitute, and dissolve committees including the Committee for Scrutiny and Evaluation of Proposals, and to define their terms of reference.
  • ✓To receive reports and recommendations from such committees and take appropriate decisions thereon.

Clarification

The Board shall nominate Trustees only to the Trust and Governing Body. Committees shall be constituted by the Governing Body and may include Trustees or other members, as deemed appropriate.

3 Core Benevolent Functions

  • ✓Based on the recommendations of the Committee constituted for the purpose, to examine, decide, and sanction cases relating to ex-gratia assistance, benevolent financial support and welfare measures for families of deceased forestry personnel and personnel who suffer serious injuries, disabilities, or critical illnesses while performing their duties.
  • ✓To take time-sensitive decisions in deserving cases without waiting for approval of the Board of Trustees.
  • ✓To determine the quantum of assistance, the mode of disbursement (lump sum or instalments), and beneficiaries among eligible family members.

4 Financial Management and Reporting

  • ✓To prepare annual statements of accounts, budgets, and financial summaries.
  • ✓To place before the Board of Trustees the annual activity report, and the audited accounts for review and record.

5 Liaison, Fund Mobilization, and Outreach

  • ✓Through a committee constituted for the purpose, liaise with State Forest Departments, PCCFs/Nodal Officers, forestry associations and donors.
  • ✓To mobilize funds consistent with the objects of the Trust.

6 Records and Transparency

  • ✓To maintain proper records of decisions taken, assistance granted, minutes of meetings, and correspondence.
  • ✓To ensure transparency, fairness, and consistency in decision-making.
